P3471 Negotiating Under Fire: How Military Escalation Has Been Used to Influence Diplomatic Talks in Ukraine (2022-2026)

Subheader - TBD

Imagine over a million people left without electricity, water, or heating while temperatures drop to -20°C. This is not the premise of a dystopian film, nor the result of a failed state unable to provide basic services. It is the reality unfolding in Europe’s largest country - Ukraine.

The winter of 2025-2026 became one of the harshest periods Ukrainians have endured since the start of Russia’s full-scale invasion. Unlike previous winters, this one brought no “quiet” intervals. Instead, it was marked by sustained and systematic attacks on critical infrastructure, particularly energy systems essential for civilian survival.

Over the three winter months alone, Russia carried out more than 16,800 strikes targeting civilian infrastructure. Power plants, electricity grids, and heating systems — the foundations of daily life — became primary targets. Ukrainian Prime Minister Denys Shmyhal and President Volodymyr Zelenskyy have both confirmed that there is not a single power plant in Ukraine that has not been attacked, with thousands of megawatts of generating capacity lost as a result.
